Defection

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 5 min read

There are five special actions (excluding basic movement, reinforcement, and route swap) available during a player's turn. One of those is defection, which can only be done during a player's turn. Like all special actions, you must pay by yielding.

About Defection

In the real world, defection is defined as "the desertion of one's country or cause in favor of an opposing one." In Vekke, the defection mechanic mirrors this in a way. One of your tokens leaves play and you pick up an enemy token in exchange.

Performing a Defection

Defection icon two overlapping circles.

As long as you have more than 2 of your unit tokens in play (on the board) and there is at least 1 enemy token in the void, the defection button will be activated. During your Action Phase, you click the button and choose which token in play you will sacrifice. Your token is yielded to the void and 1 enemy token moves from the void into your captives as if you captured it.

Examples

In the figure below, you can see that the defection icon has been activated (circled in blue on the right). There are 3 Turtle tokens in the void and 6 Dragon Tokens (also circled in blue). All active Turtle tokens are highlighted in gold dashed rings. The Phase Bar guides you: "CHOOSE A TOKEN TO SACRIFICE" (highlighted blue).

Note
You have the option to cancel this action. The button is below the board (also highlighted blue).

Defection activation: icon selected (circled), friendly tokens highlighted in gold dashed rings, phase bar prompts CHOOSE A TOKEN TO SACRIFICE

Once you select a token to sacrifice, it will be transferred to the void. The Turtle token at b3 was selected. Below you will see that it is no longer on the board. In the example below, the void also now has 4 Turtle units in the void and 5 Dragon tokens remaining (circled in blue). Note that one of the Dragon tokens has moved into Turtle player's captives.

After defection: sacrificed Turtle token (b3) moves to void; one enemy Dragon token transfers from void to your captives (void now has 5 remaining)

Strategy
Use this when you need an enemy unit for something like an early route swap or ransom and you are up on active material. Only use this for game-ending moves when you are very low on active-material unless you have enough reserves to bounce back.
Important
Grid coordinates use lower case letters. This is because capital letters mean something else in Vekke notation.
