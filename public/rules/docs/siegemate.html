Siegemate

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 5 min read

There are five ways to win in Vekke. One of the most common is by locking up all enemy units on the board. In order to get the siegemate victory, they also cannot have any reinforcements to play on their turn.

Lore
The siegemate is named after checkmate in Chess. In Chess, you win by placing the King under check until it no longer has anywhere it can move (and you cannot attack the threatening pieces).1 In Vekke, placing a unit under siege locks the piece in place, making it unable to maneuver.

Sieging Enemy Units

To do a basic siege, you must surround a token on at least 4 sides. Surrounding on only 4 sides is called a Seattle Siege.

A basic Seattle siege (4-sided surround on c4)

These can be any 4 adjacent spaces. Notice how the Kraken unit above (navy token) on c4 has 4 Stag units surrounding it (burgundy tokens).

Important
Grid coordinates use lower case letters. This is because capital letters mean something else in Vekke notation.

Variation of a Seattle siege with different adjacent positions

As you can see, Kraken above is under siege still, but the units are on different grid spaces.

Strategy
You can siege with more than 4 units, which is harder to escape from.
Lore
Surrounding enemy pieces was inspired by Go, where one of the strategies is to surround opposing pieces.2

Getting the Siegemate

If all your enemy units are under siege, this might still not result in a siegemate. This is because as long as they can place reinforcements, they are still in the game.

Note
Remember, for every route that cannot be used during a player's turn, they must yield 1 unit from their reserves to the void.

So if a player starts their turn with every piece in play under siege, they will have 3 routes they cannot use, so they must yield 3 reserves to the void. This means that if they have 3 or fewer reserves while all their units in play are under siege, you win by siegemate.

Strategy
If you can create an early siege and keep reinforcing it with more units, you can essentially hold that piece the entire game, making siegemate more likely.
Lore
Sieging and holding sieges is a dominant strategy employed by Order of the Serpent. This is because the philosophy of serpent is to constrict, first and foremost.
