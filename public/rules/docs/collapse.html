Collapse

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 5 min read

There are five ways to win in Vekke. One of the rarer ones is when your opponent cannot make a legal move during their turn and has no reserves remaining to pay the unusable route tax. This is called a collapse.

In simple terms, a collapse occurs when a player runs out of both legal moves and resources.

This condition is considered self-inflicted, because the player created the situation through poor resource management or route usage. It is different from siegemate, which occurs when an opponent deliberately locks all of your pieces under siege.

Losing By Collapse

Even though this is a win condition, it is the result of the losing player mishandling their resources.

During a turn, any routes that cannot be used must be paid for by yielding 1 unit from your reserves to the void. This is known as the unusable route tax.

Note
Remember: for every route that cannot be used during a player's turn, they must yield 1 unit from their reserves to the void.

If a player has no legal moves and cannot pay this tax, they immediately lose the game by collapse.

Collapse vs. Siegemate

Collapse is closely related to siegemate, but the two conditions are different.

  • Collapse occurs when a player causes their own loss by running out of usable moves and reserves.
  • Siegemate occurs when an opponent forces the same situation by locking all of your pieces under siege.

Both involve the unusable route tax, but the cause of the position determines the win condition.

How Collapse Happens

The most common cause of collapse is using routes on the wrong pieces and leaving yourself with unusable routes.

Example
Assume you have two units in play and two routes left to use.

You use one route on a token that moves it to space f6. Your other token is on a1, and your last remaining route is in direction 8 or 4.

In this position, neither token can legally move using that route.

The route shown, 8/3, is unusable because both units are in corner spaces

If there are no tokens remaining in your reserves, you cannot pay the unusable route tax. You would lose the game via collapse.

Strategic Considerations

Collapse is usually avoidable, but it becomes more likely when players are low on reserves or careless with route usage.

Strategy
Be mindful of where your tokens end up after each move. Poor positioning can easily leave multiple routes unusable, which can quickly drain your reserves.
Strategy
If your opponent is low on reserves, you may be able to bait them into using routes inefficiently. A tempting capture can sometimes lead them into a collapse.
Lore
Baiting opponents into collapse is one strategy employed by Order of the Fox. This is because the philosophy of fox is to achieve victory through deception and subterfuge, first and foremost.
