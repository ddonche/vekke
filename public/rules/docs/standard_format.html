Standard Format Rules

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 9 min read

Welcome to Vekke, the Game of Routes. These rules are for Standard Format. This is the simplest format, if you are looking for the easiest to learn. If you are looking for the rules for the other formats, see the bottom of the left navigation panel.

Vekke — Standard Format

Introduction

Vekke is a turn-based strategy game played on a 6×6 grid or intersectional style board; sides are typically blue (brake) and white (wake). Each turn, players must use three route markers that dictate how their tokens move. The object is to eliminate or immobilize your opponent's tokens. The game can end several ways, but the most common are by elimination — removing all your opponent's tokens — and siegemate — surrounding your opponent's tokens such that they cannot move.1

Setup

Players alternate placing one token at a time until each has placed 3 (brake places first). After placing these 3, each player has 15 tokens remaining in reserves. Reserves are the tokens on your side of the board waiting to enter play. Each player then receives 3 route markers, placed face-up on their side of the board. Place 3 route markers face-up beside the board as the route queue, with the remaining markers face-down as the route deck. Wake moves first.

Turn Sequence

Each turn follows three phases: MOVE → REINFORCE → SWAP.

Movement

You make exactly one move per route — three routes means three moves, applied to any of your tokens in any order. Each route dictates a direction (1–8) and a distance (pips). Directions use compass style from brake's perspective: 1 = north, then continuing clockwise up to 8 = northwest.

A token may never land on a friendly token. Landing on an enemy token invades it — remove it from the board and place it in your captives. Captives are enemy tokens you have taken out of play, kept on your side of the board.

A token surrounded on 4 or more adjacent spaces by enemy tokens is under siege and cannot move. A token surrounded on all 8 adjacent spaces is captured — this is called a Perfect siege. Edge spaces have only 5 adjacent spaces and therefore a Perfect siege is not possible there. Corner spaces have only 3 adjacent spaces and cannot be sieged at all. Only immediately adjacent spaces count — flanking does not create adjacency.

When a token reaches the edge of the board with distance remaining, it continues from the opposite end of the same row, column, or diagonal it is already traveling on. For example, a token moving north that exits the top of the board reappears at the bottom of the same column and keeps going. A token moving diagonally that exits one edge reappears at the opposite end of the same diagonal. The path never changes direction — it simply wraps.

If a route cannot legally be used, you must yield one token from your reserves to the Void per unusable route. The Void is a neutral out-of-play area; both players' tokens can go there. If you cannot yield (because you have no reserves), you are at risk of collapse (see Ending the Game).

Reinforcement

After you have used all your routes, place one token from your reserves onto any empty space.

Swap

Once your reinforcement is in play, choose one route from your set, discard it, and take one from the route queue. Refill the queue from the deck. This ends your turn.

Ending the Game

You win by:

  • Elimination: All enemy tokens removed.
  • Siegemate: Opponent cannot move and has no reserves.*
  • Collapse: Opponent cannot use all required routes and has no reserves to yield.
  • Resignation or timeout.

  1. Siegemate requires the opponent to have no reinforcements in reserve. ↩
