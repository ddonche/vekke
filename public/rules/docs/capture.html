Capture

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 6 min read

There are two ways to capture an enemy unit: invasion and perfect siege. Captured enemy units are removed from play and kept alongside your reserves. These tokens form the areas known as your captives. Captured tokens can be used to purchase special actions such as Early Route Swaps and Ransoms.

Note
This article is about the maneuver strategies resulting in capture; see the previously mentioned article on captives for more on the spatial area.

Invasion

The most common way to capture enemy units is to invade their space. This is done by maneuvering one of your tokens so that it concludes its movement on a space occupied by an enemy unit. In other words, you land on it. Some basics to remember:

  • only the destination space counts
  • you can't invade spaces with reinforcements
  • you can't invade spaces with recoil

When you invade an enemy unit's space, that unit is removed from the board and placed into your captives area.

Note
This is exactly the same as capturing pieces in Chess.1 The only difference is, in Chess, the piece is removed from the game. In Vekke, the captured piece moves into your captives area.

Example

In the figure below, you can see the highlighted token (Dragon on d5). You can also see that there is only 1 remaining route for this turn (circled in blue). That is route 1/3. Warning
Remember that you cannot invade during reinforcement or recoil.
Important
Grid coordinates use lower case letters. This is because capital letters mean something else in Vekke notation.

Perfect Siege

You can also capture an enemy unit by surrounding it on all 8 sides. This is called a Perfect Siege.

Perfect siege: 8-sided surround results in immediate capture (unit was in the center space)

Winning by Elimination

The most common way to win the game is by eliminating all the enemy tokens from the board.

Considerations

Since elimination is a win condition, capturing is an important part of the game. Keep a few things in mind to maximize your chances of success:

  • Capture 3 units in one turn to draft 2 of yours back from the void
  • Use early route swaps to surprise and capture off-guard
  • Avoid "hanging" your pieces (leaving them vulnerable to easy capture)
  • Place reinforcements carefully to prevent immediate invasion
  • Leverage flanking to extend reach and threaten captures
  • Use recoil to evade or reposition away from threats
Lore
Capture by invasion is a dominant strategy employed by Order of the Wolf. This is because the philosophy of wolf is to hunt down tokens, first and foremost.
