July Siege

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 6 min read

Another kind of siege is the 7-sided, or July siege. Simply surround an enemy unit on any 7 sides to perform a July.

Lore
The July siege was named after the month of July. Since it is the 7th month and July sieges involve 7 tokens.

Surround on 7 Sides

To do the July siege, you must surround a token on 7 sides.

A July siege (7-sided surround on c3 Raven by Wolf)

Any 7 adjacent spaces creates a July siege. Notice how the Raven unit above (purple token) on c3 has 7 Wolf units surrounding it (blue tokens).

Important
Grid coordinates use lower case letters. This is because capital letters mean something else in Vekke notation.

Variation: Turtle under 7-sided siege on b4 by Spider

As you can see, Turtle (bronze token) above (b4) is under siege on 7 sides by Spider (black tokens) in a different variation from the previous example.

Strategy
Remember, you can end the game if all enemy tokens are under siege and they have no reinforcements to place on their turn. This is called a siegemate.

Sieged Tokens Become Locked

Any token that is under siege is locked. It cannot be moved until the siege is broken or the opponent abandons it (by moving one of the sieging units away). A sieged unit will show that it is locked by having a dashed outline around it.

July Sieges are Stable

Because Seattle sieges involve only 4 units, they are easiest to break. You can "rescue" a sieged token by simply capturing one of the 4 "siegers". To rescue a sieged token that's under July, you must remove 4 enemy tokens. This makes it stronger than the basic Seattle or the Ikeda.

July is Harder to Abandon

Because July sieges involve 7 tokens, you can move 3 units away if you need to while maintaining the siege. This is not the case with 4-sided sieges:

If a player only has 4 units in play, and they all engaged in a siege, at least one unit must "abandon" the siege to use all the routes on their turn. This breaks the siege, at least temporarily. If the siege is to be maintained, one of two things must happen:

  • the player must try to maneuver in such a way as to return the "roving" unit back to the siege
  • the player must put a reinforcement to create the siege again

Thus, there is less risk involved with the 7-sided siege. You have more options.

Dangers

There are some dangers, however, with the July siege (and other sieges). Take a look at the example below. Turtle player took advantage of the grouping to create sieges of its own. Tight groupings like this can create multiple simultaneous sieges (benefiting both players), making the position volatile and prone to rapid swings.

Dangers of clustering: multiple overlapping sieges in tight space

About Perfect Siege

Since a July is only 1 space away from a Perfect Siege, which surrounds a unit on all 8 sides, it is important to note that perfectly sieged units become automatically captured.

You can see here the July and the highlighted token, which will be moving along route 7/2 to form the Perfect.

July in place; next move completes Perfect Siege and captures the token

Once the eighth unit moves into position, Turtle's unit is captured.

Strategy
If you do not want the token captured, do not move beyond a July.

Perfectly sieged token is automatically captured

Edge Units

You cannot siege an edge unit with a July. Units on the edge of the board cannot be sieged by more than 5 tokens, as there are only 5 adjacent spaces. This means an Ikeda is the biggest siege you can do on an edge unit. Below you see the Kraken unit on f5 under siege this way.
