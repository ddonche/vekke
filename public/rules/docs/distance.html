Distance

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 5 min read

Vekke is the 'Game of Routes'. Unlike chess, which has 6 different pieces, each with their own movement patterns, Vekke's units are represented by identical tokens. Movement is dictated by routes. There are 28 unique routes, each with a pairing of both direction and distance. Players use these routes to move the tokens according to the markings on each route icon.

The 4 Distances

Vekke is played on a 6x6 grid board. There are 4 possible distances you can move. These are annotated on the route markers with pips indicating how far you can move a unit token. These are visually distinct from direction, which is represented by arrows. See below for each distance:

  • 1 pip = move 1 space (all 8 directions)
  • 2 pips = move 2 spaces (all 8 directions)
  • 3 pips = move 3 spaces (all 8 directions)
  • 4 pips = move 4 spaces (only orthagonal; up, down, left, right)

A diagram of every route marker; distance is shown in pips on the bottom half

Flanking

Unlike chess, the edge of the board in Vekke is not the end of movement. If you reach the edge of the board and have distance remaining, you continue in the same column, row, or diagonal from the opposite side of the board. This is called flanking.

Examples

In the diagram below, you can see the Turtle unit on b2 highlighted. If it uses the 7/2 route, which is circled in blue, it will reach the edge of the board after 1 space, but it has 1 space remaining.

Before flanking: Turtle on b2; route 7/2 (circled in blue) allows 2 spaces west

Now you can see that the unit continues in the same row from the other side of the board and ends up on f2 (see the highlighted token below.

After flanking: Turtle wraps around edge and lands on f2 (highlighted)

Warning
Routes must be used fully—you cannot stop short or partial-move. Units jump over intervening spaces (no touching mid-path). You cannot land on your own unit. Landing on an enemy triggers invasion (capture).

Notation

Route notation is always done with direction first, followed by distance. So the route 7/2 is in the direction of 7 (West) for 2 spaces.

Important
Grid coordinates use lower case letters. This is because capital letters mean something else in Vekke notation.
Strategy
Some routes will land you in the same spot as other routes. For example, a route that takes you 4 spaces in one direction is essentially the same as a route that takes you 2 spaces in the opposite direction. Be aware of this. Sometimes you may want this and other times you may not. For example, a 4-space east route (3/4) lands you in the same spot as a 2-space west route (7/2) from the opposite direction. This can be useful for repositioning or redundant for surprise—be aware of both options.
