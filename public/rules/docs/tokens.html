Tokens

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 6 min read

In Vekke, the game pieces used to represent units are referred to as tokens. They are also sometimes called pieces. Both terms mean the same thing and can be used interchangeably.

Token Description

Unlike Chess, which has 6 different pieces (King, Queen, Rook, Bishop, Knight, Pawn) that each have different movement and capture rules, Vekke is more like Go or Checkers, where all the pieces on either side are identical. Each side in Vekke has 18 identical pieces called tokens. The default tokens that come with physical sets and appear in player inventories on the Vekke website are teal and white Go-style stones.

Lore
Early versions of Vekke were called Betaori after Beta Orionis (Rigel), the brightest star in Orion. Because the star shines both blue and white sometimes, the original game had blue and white tokens. This is why default tokens are still blue (teal) and white.

A grid style board with both teal and white default tokens

Other Tokens

Even though Vekke comes with teal and white default tokens, anything can be used as a token set as long as each player's tokens are distinguishable from their opponent's. You don't have to use Go-style stones. Other options include:

  • checkers
  • beads
  • Pente stones
  • coins
  • discs

Tokens can be made from many materials, including resin, glass, plastic, wood, or metal.

Important
You do not have to use blue and white variations. This is another bonus of Vekke's customizable nature. The only rule is that each player must have visually distinct tokens from their opponent's. It is recommended that each player use tokens with clearly different colors or designs. (see below on why color is not a factor)

This enables players to get creative or use tokens that match their personality. Take a look at the image below. One side has dartboard tokens and the other is using donuts!

A grid style board with dartboard and donut tokens

Colors

One thing that sometimes confuses new players is the use of B and W notation to represent each player. Since the default tokens are blue and white, new players often assume that B means blue and W means white.

This used to be the case, initially.

But with the current ruleset, it was decided to allow players to use any color of token, and any kind of token (within reason; some tokens might be offensive to some players).

Lore
When this change went into effect, player sides changed from white and blue to wake and brake. This enabled the game to keep using W and B in annotation.

Obtaining Other Token Sets

The website offers several ways to add new token sets to your inventory. One way is to complete achievements, which either unlock new token sets, route markers, or even board designs. Another way is to purchase them from the marketplace. Players with a pro account receive a monthly "drop", which may include new token sets.

The easiest and quickest way, however, is to join an Order. Joining an Order is free, and you gain instant access to that Order's unique token set. Each Order's token set features its sigil and color combination.

A grid style board with blue Wolf and red Spider tokens

Note
You can switch Orders anytime you like, just remember that you also switch Order tokens when you do.

Outside of the online game, you may want to get new tokens for your physical set. Feel free to experiment and get creative. Try using game pieces from other games, or you may even find custom Vekke token sets sold by retailers and hobbyists. Finally, you can also have custom tokens made to suit your personality and tastes.
