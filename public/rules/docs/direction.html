Direction

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 5 min read

Vekke is the 'Game of Routes'. Unlike chess, which has 6 different pieces, each with their own movement patterns, Vekke's units are represented by identical tokens. Movement is dictated by routes. There are 28 unique routes, each with a pairing of both direction and distance. Players use these routes to move the tokens according to the markings on each route icon.

The 8 Directions

Vekke is played on a 6x6 grid board. There are 8 possible directions you can move. These are annotated on the route markers with an arrow pointing in the direction of movement. These are visually distinct from distance, which is represented by pips. In all formats except Explorer, directions are numbered 1 through 8, with 1 being up (North).

A diagram of every route marker; direction is shown in arrows on the top half

See below for each direction:

  • 1 is up (North)
  • 2 is up-right (Northeast)
  • 3 is right (East)
  • 4 is down-right (Southeast)
  • 5 is down (South)
  • 6 is down-left (Southwest)
  • 7 is left (West)
  • 8 is up-left (Northwest)

A grid style board with all 8 directions annotated, showing in red.

Note
In the physical game, directions are from B brake side. In Vekke, we say "8 is NW from brake". The board will have a blue border on one side and white border on the other to know which side is which. Also note that all route markers must be situated so that the arrow icons are towards the W wake player. This is so there is no ambiguity as to which direction the route intends.

Notation

Route notation is always done with direction first, followed by distance. So the route 7/2 is in the direction of 7 (West) for 2 spaces.

Explorer Format

In Explorer Format, you have the option of randomizing the directions. To do so, you simply use direction markers, which you place around the board to show what each new direction is.

You need full pips for this
Note that to use this format, you will need route markers with pips on both sides (direction and distance). This format adds replayability and strategic uncertainty by redefining direction mappings each game.
