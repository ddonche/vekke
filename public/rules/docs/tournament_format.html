Tournament Format Rules

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 7 min read

Welcome to Vekke, the Game of Routes. These rules are for Tournament Format. This format is the only format used in Tournament play. If you are looking for the rules for the other formats, see the bottom of the left navigation panel.

Introduction

Tournament format is the full game. If you know Standard, you already know the foundation — the same 6×6 grid, the same win conditions, and a similar turn structure. What Tournament adds is economy. You now have tools to spend your captives and reserves on tactical advantages, a defensive reaction maneuver called Recoil, and a Mulligan phase before the game begins. Learn to use these well and you will find Standard feels like a warm-up.

Setup

Setup follows the same procedure as Standard, with one addition: the Mulligan.

Players alternate placing one token at a time until each has placed 3 (brake places first), leaving 15 tokens in reserve. Each player is then dealt 3 route markers, placed face-up on their side of the board. Place 3 route markers face-up beside the board as the route queue, with the remaining markers face-down as the route deck.

Mulligan

Before the first turn begins, each player may mulligan. To mulligan, return one token from the board to your reserves, then discard any or all of your routes and draw that many replacements from the deck. Each player may mulligan up to twice — once per token returned. After two mulligans you have only one token in play, so a third mulligan would concede the game.

Wake moves first.

Turn Sequence

Each turn follows three phases: ACTION → REINFORCE → SWAP.

Action

During your Action phase, you move using your routes and may also perform Special Actions. You make exactly one move per route — three routes means three moves, applied to any of your tokens in any order. Special Actions can be taken at any time during your Action phase, before, between, or after your moves, as long as their costs and conditions are met. You may perform each Special Action at most once per turn.

A sieged token cannot move by any means, including Recoil.

Special Actions

Special Actions are optional and cost tokens to perform. You may perform any number of different Special Actions during your Action phase, provided you can pay for each, but no individual Special Action more than once per turn.

Extra Reinforcement — Yield 2 tokens from your reserves to the Void. If you do, place 2 reinforcements this turn instead of 1.

Early Route Swap — Yield 2 captured enemy tokens from your captives to the Void. You may then swap one unused route from your set with one from the queue immediately. The new route must be used during this turn. You cannot use Early Route Swap after all of your routes have already been used. You do not get a free swap at the end of your turn if you use this action.

Ransom — Yield 2 captured enemy tokens from your captives to the Void to return 1 of your own tokens from the Void to your reserves. If you have no tokens in the Void, this action is unavailable.

Movement

Each route dictates a direction (1–8) and a distance (pips). Directions use compass style from brake's perspective: 1 = north, then continuing clockwise up to 8 = northwest.

A token may never land on a friendly token. Landing on an enemy token invades it — remove it from the board and place it in your captives. Captives are enemy tokens you have taken out of play, kept on your side of the board.

A token surrounded on 4 or more adjacent spaces by enemy tokens is sieged and cannot move. A token surrounded on all 8 adjacent spaces is captured — this is called a Perfect siege. Edge spaces have only 5 adjacent spaces and therefore a Perfect siege is not possible there. Corner spaces have only 3 adjacent spaces and cannot be sieged at all. Only immediately adjacent spaces count — flanking does not create adjacency.

When a token reaches the edge of the board with distance remaining, it continues from the opposite end of the same row, column, or diagonal it is already traveling on. For example, a token moving north that exits the top of the board reappears at the bottom of the same column and keeps going. A token moving diagonally that exits one edge reappears at the opposite end of the same diagonal. The path never changes direction — it simply wraps.

If a route cannot legally be used, yield one token from your reserves to the Void per unusable route. If you cannot yield, you are at risk of collapse (see Ending the Game).

Draft — If you invade 3 enemy tokens in a single turn, return up to 2 of your own tokens from the Void to your reserves. If there is only 1 of your tokens in the Void, return 1. If there are none, you return none. There is no choice — you always return the maximum available.

Reinforcement

After you have finished your Action phase — all moves and any Special Actions — place one token from your reserves onto any empty space. If you paid for an Extra Reinforcement, place 2.

Swap

If you paid for an Early Route Swap during your Action phase, you do not swap again. Otherwise, once your reinforcements are in play, choose one route from your set, discard it, and take one from the queue. Refill the queue from the deck. This ends your turn.

At any time during your turn, you may ask your opponent to shuffle the route deck. The queue itself is never shuffled.

Recoil

Each player may perform only one action during their opponent's turn: a Recoil.

To perform a Recoil, yield 1 token from your reserves and 1 captive to the Void. You then move 1 friendly token 1 space onto any empty adjacent space.

You may recoil before or immediately after any single opponent move, or after your opponent places reinforcements. If your opponent makes another move before you declare, that window closes.

You can recoil before a token would be taken, but if it is captured, you cannot use recoil to "undo" that capture.

Turn One Exception

A player may perform Recoil on turn 1 during their opponent's turn without paying its cost if they have only one token left in play.

Ending the Game

You win by:

  • Elimination: All enemy tokens removed.
  • Siegemate: Opponent cannot move and has no reserves.*
  • Collapse: Opponent cannot use all required routes and has no reserves to yield.
  • Resignation or timeout.

There is no draw in Vekke. Play continues until one player is the victor.

Siegemate requires the opponent to have no reinforcements in reserve.
