Worthington Siege

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 5 min read

Another kind of siege is the 6-sided, or Worthington siege. Simply surround an enemy unit on any 6 sides to perform a Worthington.

Surround on 6 Sides

To do the Worthington siege, you must surround a token on 6 sides.

A Worthington siege (6-sided surround on c3)

Any 6 adjacent spaces creates a Worthington siege. Notice how the Stag unit above (burgundy token) on c3 has 6 Kraken units surrounding it (navy tokens).

Important
Grid coordinates use lower case letters. This is because capital letters mean something else in Vekke notation.

Variation of a Worthington siege with different adjacent positions

As you can see, Stag above is under siege still, but the sieging units are on different grid spaces.

Strategy
Remember, you can end the game if all enemy tokens are under siege and they have no reinforcements to place on their turn. This is called a siegemate.

Sieged Tokens Become Locked

Any token that is under siege is locked. It cannot be moved until the siege is broken or the opponent abandons it (by moving one of the sieging units away). A sieged unit will show that it is locked by having a dashed outline around it.

Worthington Sieges are Stable

Because Seattle sieges involve only 4 units, they are easiest to break. You can "rescue" a sieged token by simply capturing one of the 4 "siegers". To rescue a sieged token that's under Worthington, you must remove 3 enemy tokens. This makes it stronger than the basic Seattle or the Ikeda.

Worthington is Harder to Abandon

Because Worthington sieges involve 6 tokens, you can move two units away if you need to while maintaining the siege. This is not the case with 4-sided sieges:

If a player only has 4 units in play, and they all engaged in a siege, at least one unit must "abandon" the siege to use all the routes on their turn. This breaks the siege, at least temporarily. If the siege is to be maintained, one of two things must happen:

  • the player must try to maneuver in such a way as to return the "roving" unit back to the siege
  • the player must put a reinforcement to create the siege again

Thus, there is less risk involved with the 6-sided siege. You have more options.

Edge Units

Worthington sieges are impossible on edge units, as they have only 5 adjacent spaces (maximum Ikeda siege; see example on f5). Below you see the Kraken unit on f5 under siege this way.

Edge-position siege limitation (maximum Ikeda/5-sided on f5; Worthington impossible)
