[[double-siege]]

Double Siege

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 5 min read

It is possible to produce two simultaneous sieges with one strategic unit placement.

Note
This covers the double siege, but clustering can yield 3+ simultaneous sieges (see 'Dangers' example below for a 4-siege cluster).

Siege Basic Recap

To perform a siege, you simply need to surround an enemy token on at least 4 adjacent spaces.

A basic Seattle siege (4-sided surround on c4)

Strategy
Remember, you can end the game if all enemy tokens are under siege and they have no reinforcements to place on their turn. This is called a siegemate.
Important
Grid coordinates use lower case letters. This is because capital letters mean something else in Vekke notation.

Sieged Tokens Become Locked

Any token that is under siege is locked. It cannot be moved until the siege is broken or the opponent abandons it (by moving one of the sieging units away). A sieged unit will show that it is locked by having a dashed outline around it.

Examples

In the example below, Serpent player has 1 reinforcement to place (highlighted up top in blue).

[[image:double_siege_before.png|full|center|Before double siege: Serpent's 1 reinforcement (highlighted blue); placing at c4 locks both Fox units]]

If Serpent places the reinforcement at c4, both Fox units at b5 and c3 will be under siege. You can see that this is the case in the following image.

After placement at c4: Double siege created—Fox units at b5 and c3 now locked (dashed outlines)

Tip
This example covers how to achieve the double siege through reinforcements, but it can also be done through maneuver.

Dangers

Double sieges amplify clustering risks: tight groupings can create multiple overlapping sieges for both players, leading to volatile, swingy positions.

Clustering dangers: 4 overlapping sieges in tight space (benefits both players)
