Seattle Siege

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 5 min read

The most basic kind of siege is the 4-sided, or Seattle siege. This is because to perform a siege, a minimum of 4 units must be adjacent to an enemy token.

Surround on 4 Sides

To do a basic siege, you must surround a token on at least 4 sides.

A basic Seattle siege (4-sided surround on c4)

These can be any 4 adjacent spaces. Notice how the Kraken unit above (navy token) on c4 has 4 Stag units surrounding it (burgundy tokens).

Important
Grid coordinates use lower case letters. This is because capital letters mean something else in Vekke notation.

Variation of a Seattle siege with different adjacent positions

As you can see, Kraken above is under siege still, but the units are on different grid spaces.

Strategy
Remember, you can end the game if all enemy tokens are under siege and they have no reinforcements to place on their turn. This is called a siegemate.

Sieged Tokens Become Locked

Any token that is under siege is locked. It cannot be moved until the siege is broken or the opponent abandons it (by moving one of the sieging units away). A sieged unit will show that it is locked by having a dashed outline around it.

Seattle Sieges are Flimsy

Because Seattle sieges involve only 4 units, they are easiest to break. You can "rescue" a sieged token by simply capturing one of the 4 "siegers".

Siege broken by capturing a sieging unit (Kraken invades b4)

In the figure above, Kraken has invaded b4 (from d6 with route 6/2) and captured Stag's unit there.

Seattles are Easy to Abandon

If a player only has 4 units in play, and they all engaged in a siege, at least one unit must "abandon" the siege to use all the routes on their turn. This breaks the siege, at least temporarily. If the siege is to be maintained, one of two things must happen:

  • the player must try to maneuver in such a way as to return the "roving" unit back to the siege
  • the player must put a reinforcement to create the siege again

Corners Cannot Be Sieged

Corners (a1, a6, f1, f6) have only three possible adjacent spaces, therefore they cannot be sieged. Below, you see that the Kraken unit in the upper lefthand corner on A6 is totally surrounded by Stag, yet it is not under siege. That unit can still maneuver.

Corner position fully surrounded but not under siege (only 3 adjacents)

Strengthening the Seattle

Since the Seattle siege is easily diffused, the only way to strengthen it is to add units to reinforce the siege. This will, of course, no longer be a Seattle siege if it involves 5 or more units (see Ikeda Siege).
