Captives

By Intl. Vekke Council ~ 5 min read

There are two ways to capture an enemy unit. These are invasion and perfect siege. Captured enemy units are removed from play and kept alongside your reserves. These tokens form the areas known as your captives.

Note
This article is about the area and its considerations; see the previously mentioned articles for more on those topics.

The Captives Area

"Captives" is what is referred to as where you keep your captured enemy unit tokens. These are typically placed on your side of the board, along with your reserve unit tokens. Think of these like captured chess pieces.

The reason this is important is because unit tokens are simply transferred from area to area during gameplay. There are four areas:

  • board
  • reserves
  • captives
  • void

Where this is relevant here is how units may move into your captives and out of your captives area. In the figure below you can see the player on the left has 6 enemy unit tokens (Turtle; bronze) in its captives area. The player on the right has 2 enemy unit tokens (Spider; black) in its captives area.

Example captives areas: left player (6 Turtle bronze tokens captured), right player (2 Spider black tokens captured), circled in blue.

Capturing Tokens

You collect enemy unit tokens by capturing them. We already mentioned that this is done through invasion and through performing a perfect siege. We will not be detailing the mechanics to those here. Just know that "capture" means you remove an enemy token from play and transfer it into your captives area.

Yielding Captives

Captive tokens can be used during your Action Phase to "purchase" special actions. This is calling yielding. Essentially, you give up (yield) captured tokens. These are transferred to the void area.

Yielding 2 captured enemy tokens to the void allows purchase of:

  • Early Route Swap: gain an early route swap
  • Ransom: return 1 friendly token from void to reserves.

Considerations

There are a number of things to consider when yielding captives. Do you need the route swap now? Will you need a reserve? Are you running low on material and want a reinforcement? Can you afford to give your opponent possible draft material?

Strategy
Remember that your opponent may also yield your unit tokens to void. What this means is that once your tokens are in the void, you have ways to draft or ransom them back into your reserves. This means that your opponent can do the same when you purchase early route swaps or ransoms, so pay attention.
Lore
Some styles (like the Order of the Turtle) thrives on depriving the opponent from accessing their tokens after capture (also see defection).

This mutual yielding creates tension: capturing tokens gives you resources, but leaves your own vulnerable to permanent denial if the opponent yields them first.
